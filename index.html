Content

We are a community that shares our curiosity and fascination for Space. Our aim is to bring together everyone who follows Space exploration and research, who wants to learn and share knowledge in this field. In this about us article about Space, we would like to explain what we do and what we can offer about Space. Our site offers articles and videos covering topics such as Space exploration, observations, science and technology news. We also have a large library of information about planets, stars, galaxies and other celestial bodies. In addition, we aim to increase interest in Space with Space observation guides, photo galleries and other interactive content. With the contribution of our site visitors, we are building a community to learn and explore more about Space. Space is an endless journey of discovery. Our website is a reflection of our curiosity about Space and we would be happy to welcome everyone who wants to share this curiosity.

    - MISSION DETAIL -


    No Mission Definition Date
    1 TROPICS First Launch NASA constellation of four storms tracking CubeSats will study tropical cyclones starting with the 2023 Atlantic hurricane season. TROPICS will observe the atmosphere to increase our understanding of hurricanes, typhoons and other intense weather conditions. There will be two CubeSats at launch; and two more will be released about a month later. May 2022
    2 NASA's Boeing Crew Flight Test The Crew Flight Test will demonstrate the ability of Starliner and United Launch Alliance's Atlas V rocket to safely transport astronauts to and from the International Space Station. Following a successful test flight with astronauts, NASA will begin the final process of certifying the Starliner spacecraft and systems for regular crew rotation flights to the space station. July 2022
    3 EUCLID Euclid, a European Space Agency (ESA) mission with contributions from NASA, was designed to explore the composition and evolution of the dark universe. The spacecraft will be launched aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Cape Canaveral. January 2021
    4 ASTROS Launched from Antarctica, the Astrophysics Stratospheric Telescope for High Spectral Resolution Observations at Submillimeter Wavelengths (ASTHROS) will use a balloon larger than a football field to study a phenomenon that inhibits star formation in some galaxies. December 2020
    5 NASA's SpaceX Crew-7 Launch For the mission, NASA astronaut Jasmin Moghbeli and European Space Agency astronaut Andreas Mogensen will serve as spacecraft commander and pilot, respectively. The mission is expected to launch before 2023 on a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket from Launch Complex 39A at NASA's Kennedy Space Center in Florida. Moghbeli, Mogensen and additional mission experts will join an expedition team on the space station. August 2019
    6 OSIRIS-REx delivers sample of asteroid Bennu to Earth A capsule containing fragments of asteroid Bennu will separate from the OSIRIS-REx spacecraft and enter Earth's atmosphere. The capsule will parachute into the Utah Test and Training Range in Utah's Western Desert. September 2018
